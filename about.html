Biography

One of the most well-known, if anonymous, street artists working today is Banksy (British, born c. 1974). Banksy, the alias adopted by the artist, preserves his anonymity and remains largely unknown to the public the specifics of his life. Initially, he gained fame for his graffiti, which incorporates spray paint and stenciling methods, infused with satirical social commentary and humor, with advertising, political and contemporary imagery. During the mid-1990s, his work first started appearing on walls in and around Bristol and the Shoreditch district of London, and since then he has placed photographs on the sides of corporate buildings, billboards, and the wall of the Israeli West Bank.

The release of a new Banksy work often includes pranks or performance. For eg, in museums such as the Tate Modern in London or the Paris Louvre, he secretly added his own pieces, sneaking them in under a coat and discreetly adding them to a wall. He also opened gallery shows to the public with specially-bred rats running around the room, and once inserted into California's Disneyland theme park an inflatable doll dressed as a Guantanamo Bay prisoner.

Banksy created a large-scale installation in 2015, titled Dismaland Bemusement Park, an interactive and fully functional amusement park featuring political commentary on the distribution of wealth, sexism, and the government of the United Kingdom. The park ran between August and September 2015, and was financed by Banksy alone. Banksy produces works for many charities, in addition to his daily graffiti activity, and regularly opens exhibitions to diverse audiences and critical acclaim. He lives and works in England.
