Banksy said that he was inspired by 3D, a graffiti artist and founding member of the musical group Massive Attack.

How he rose to fame

Banksy, with two other artists known as Kato and Tes, began as a freehand graffiti artist in 1990-1994 as part of Bristol's DryBreadZ Crew (DBZ). He was influenced by local artists and with Nick Walker, Inkie and 3D, his work was part of the wider Bristol underground scene. During this time he met Steve Lazarides, a photographer from Bristol, who began selling Banksy's work and later became his agent. By 2000, after discovering how much less time it took to finish a work, he had turned to the art of stenciling. He says he turned to stencilling while hiding under a rubbish lorry from the police, when he found the stencilled serial number and he soon became more widely noticed for his art around Bristol and London by using this technique. In the 1990s, he was the goalie for the Easton Cowboys and Cowgirls football team, and in 2001, he toured Mexico with the club. The Mild Mild West, painted in 1997 to cover ads for a former lawyer's office on Stokes Croft in Bristol, was Banksy's first known large wall mural. It shows a teddy bear lobbing three riot police with a Molotov cocktail.

Banksy's stencils, often paired with slogans, feature striking and humorous images. Typically, the message is anti-war, anti-capitalist, or anti-establishment. Rats, apes, policemen, soldiers, girls, and the elderly are often the topics.

In July 2011, after the premises became a Muslim cultural centre, one of Banksy's early pieces, The Hedgehog, which had been a prominent landmark on the exterior wall of a former social club in Eastville for over ten years, was inadvertently painted over.
